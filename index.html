Research Interests

Below you can find my current research interests.

Galaxy Evolution from High-Redshift to Low-Redshift

I try to understand how galaxies evolve from their clumpy and highly star-forming to the more passive systems we today in the Local Universe.

The Baryon Cycle in Galaxies

Stars form from cold clumps of molecular gas, which, in turn, form through the accretion of atomic gas. I seek to understand the interplay between stars and these different gas phases.

Collaborations

The MAGPI Survey

The Middle Ages Galaxy Properties in Integral Field Spectroscopy (MAGPI) Survey is an European Southern Observatory Large Program on the Very Large Telescope with the goal of trying to understand what aspects of galaxy evolution change their dynamics.
